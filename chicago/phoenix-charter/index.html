THE PHOENIX CHARTER

Starts at the Cultural Center, the Loop
3 chapters · 2 to 6 hours · pause anytime
Best for groups of 2–5 · ages 10+

In October 1871, Chicago burned — and in the vaults beneath the courthouse, the city's memory burned with it. Every deed. Every title. Every record of who owned what. The official cause was a cow, a lantern, a widow. That story was invented by a reporter who later confessed. The men who rebuilt this city knew it. They kept a truer account — and hid it in plain sight.
